# OpenHands Champions Program (v1 / 2026)

## Purpose

The OpenHands Champions Program recognizes community members who meaningfully contribute to the OpenHands ecosystem through building, sharing, testing, and product feedback.

The program is designed to be:

- lightweight
- meaningful
- sustainable

Champions are **volunteers**, not employees, contractors, or unpaid labor.

## Program principles

- **Low ceremony, high legitimacy.** Participation should be simple, but earning recognition should mean something.
- **Artifact over activity.** Champions are recognized for clear contributions, not for constant engagement.
- **Voluntary and opt-in.** No one is expected to perform ongoing unpaid work.
- **Seasonal by design.** Each year is a new season. Champions can opt in again, skip a season, or participate in one or both tracks.
- **Publicly verifiable.** This repo is the source of truth for official Champions.
- **Community-first.** Champions own their work, voice, projects, and content.

## Season structure

The Champions Program runs in annual seasons.

- **2026 = Season 1**
- Champions can join on a rolling basis during the season.

At the end of each season:

- Champion status for that season is finalized.
- Badges are awarded for completed tracks.
- Participants may opt in again for the next season.

## Tracks

There are two Champion tracks:

1. **Hacker**
2. **Insider**

Participants may join either track or both.

## Track 1: Hacker

### Who this is for

Hackers are builders who create public projects using OpenHands.

They use the current/live version of OpenHands to build something interesting, useful, experimental, educational, or creative.

### Commitment (to complete the track)

- Build **one** custom project with OpenHands.
- Publish a blog post / write-up / project explanation.
- Share a demo with the community (live or recorded).
- Submit a PR to this repo adding the project.

### Verification

This repo is the source of truth.

A Hacker is considered official for the season once:

- their project is added to the champions list, and
- the PR is reviewed and merged.

> Note: the Champion must submit the PR themselves.

### Recognition

Once verified, the participant receives:

- OpenHands **Hacker** badge for the season
- inclusion in the champions list
- potential promotion via OpenHands social/community channels
- eligibility for swag, merch, credits, or other discretionary benefits

## Track 2: Insider

### Who this is for

Insiders are trusted community members who help shape the future of OpenHands.

They provide feedback on beta experiences, upcoming product directions, developer workflows, and real-world usage.

### Commitment (to complete the track)

- Participate in beta testing or product feedback opportunities.
- Attend at least **4** feedback sessions during the season.
- Provide honest, direct, unfiltered feedback to the OpenHands team.

### Attendance and verification

- Each feedback session includes a unique attendance form shared during the call.
- Attendance is tracked through form submissions.

An Insider is considered official for the season when:

- they have attended at least 4 qualifying feedback sessions, and
- participation has been verified.

> Insider attendance details are not public. Only verified status is public.

### Recognition

Once verified, the participant receives:

- OpenHands **Insider** badge for the season
- inclusion in the champions list
- direct feedback opportunities with product, engineering, maintainers, and leadership
- potential access to beta testing opportunities
- potential promotion, swag, merch, credits, or other discretionary benefits

## Badges

Each completed track receives a unique seasonal badge.

Badges should include:

- OpenHands branding
- track name
- season/year
- reference to the official champions list as the source of truth

Badges may be PNGs, GIFs, SVGs, or other lightweight image assets.

The champions list remains the verification mechanism.

## Benefits (may vary)

Champions may receive:

- seasonal badges
- inclusion in the champions list
- promotion across OpenHands community and social channels
- swag or merch
- OpenHands credits
- direct access to maintainers, engineers, product, leadership, or DevRel
- beta testing opportunities
- early feedback opportunities

Benefits are discretionary and may vary by season, region, availability, and program capacity.

## What this program is not

The Champions Program is not:

- an unpaid labor program
- a social posting requirement
- an ambassador quota system
- a job or contractor relationship
- a moderation team
- a guaranteed benefits program
- a requirement to constantly engage
